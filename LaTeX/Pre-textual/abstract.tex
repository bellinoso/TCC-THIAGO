\begin{foreignabstract}

This work presents the development of an interactive web-based simulator for the kinematic analysis of a six-degree-of-freedom (6-DOF) robotic manipulator, designed to support teaching in automation and robotics courses. The tool, executed directly in the browser, implements modeling based on the Denavit–Hartenberg convention, enabling both forward and inverse kinematics, as well as trajectory planning through cubic polynomial interpolation. The system allows individual joint control or Cartesian pose control of the end-effector, in addition to real-time visualization of the homogeneous transformation matrix and plots of joint position, velocity, and angular acceleration.


\end{foreignabstract}
